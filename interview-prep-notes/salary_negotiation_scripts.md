# Salary Negotiation Scripts (Without Revealing ₹10L)

**For: 4+ Years Backend Engineer | Current: ₹10L | Target: ₹25L-₹35L**

---

## 🎯 Core Negotiation Strategy

**Your Situation:**
- Current: ₹10L (severely underpaid)
- Target: ₹28L-₹35L (market rate for your experience)
- **Challenge:** If you reveal ₹10L, they'll lowball you at ₹18L-₹22L

**Strategy:**
1. **NEVER volunteer your current salary**
2. **Deflect questions about current salary**
3. **Anchor to market rate** (₹28L-₹35L)
4. **Create competition** (multiple offers)
5. **Be willing to walk away** (you have ₹10L backup)

---

## 📞 Round 1: Initial Recruiter Screen

### **Scenario 1: "What's your current CTC?"**

**❌ BAD Answer:**
> "I'm currently at ₹10 lakhs per annum"

**Why bad:** They'll offer ₹18L-₹20L ("We're doubling your salary!")

---

**✅ GOOD Answer (Option A - Deflect):**
> "I'm currently employed at a product company, but I'm looking for a role that offers market rate compensation for my experience level. Based on my research, for 4+ years backend engineers with microservices experience, the range is ₹28-35 lakhs. Is that within your budget for this role?"

**Why good:**
- ✅ Doesn't reveal ₹10L
- ✅ Anchors to ₹28L-₹35L
- ✅ Turns question back to them
- ✅ Shows you know the market

---

**✅ GOOD Answer (Option B - Honest but Strategic):**
> "I'm at ₹10 lakhs currently, but that's significantly below market rate. My current company is known to pay below market, which is exactly why I'm looking to move. I'm targeting ₹28-35 lakhs, which aligns with market rate for 4+ years backend engineers. What's your budget for this role?"

**Why good:**
- ✅ Honest (builds trust)
- ✅ Explains the gap (not your fault)
- ✅ Reanchors to ₹28L-₹35L
- ✅ Shows confidence

**Recruiter's likely response:**
> "₹28-35L is a bit high. Our range is ₹22-28L for SDE-2."

**Your Response:**
> "I appreciate the transparency. However, ₹22L is only 2.2x my current salary which is already below market. For my experience level - 4+ years with microservices, Kafka, and distributed systems - I've seen offers in the ₹28-35L range from companies like [Razorpay/PhonePe/Swiggy]. Would you be able to match that if I perform well in interviews?"

---

### **Scenario 2: "What are your salary expectations?"**

**❌ BAD Answer:**
> "I'm flexible" or "Whatever is market rate"

**Why bad:** Vague, shows weakness

---

**✅ GOOD Answer:**
> "Based on my research and conversations with peers at companies like Razorpay, PhonePe, and Swiggy, the market rate for 4+ years backend engineers with microservices and Kafka experience is ₹28-35 lakhs. Given my background maintaining 200+ microservices and proven track record with performance optimization, I'm targeting the ₹30-35L range. Does that align with your budget?"

**Why good:**
- ✅ Specific range (₹30-35L)
- ✅ Backed by research
- ✅ Mentions peer companies (creates FOMO)
- ✅ Ties to your achievements

---

### **Scenario 3: "We need to see payslips to verify current salary"**

**Context:** Some companies ask for payslips (mostly older companies, less common in startups)

**❌ BAD Response:**
> "Okay, here are my payslips"

**Why bad:** Reveals ₹10L, instant lowball

---

**✅ GOOD Response (Option A - Politely Decline):**
> "I'm not comfortable sharing payslips as it contains sensitive financial information. However, I'm happy to provide employment verification letters or other documents. My current salary is significantly below market, which is why I'm making this move. I'm looking for ₹30-35L based on market rate for my skills and experience."

**Why good:**
- ✅ Maintains privacy
- ✅ Offers alternative (employment letter)
- ✅ Reanchors to target

**If they insist:**
> "I understand some companies have this policy, but I've received offers from [Company X] and [Company Y] without providing payslips. If this is a hard requirement, I may need to prioritize other opportunities. Is there flexibility here?"

**Translation:** "I have other options. Your call."

---

**✅ GOOD Response (Option B - Provide with Context):**
> "I can provide payslips, but want to set context. My current company (Copart India) pays significantly below market - ₹10 lakhs for 4+ years backend work. This is why I'm actively looking. The market rate for my experience level is ₹28-35 lakhs, which I've confirmed through offers from [Company X] and conversations with peers. I'm hoping you'll evaluate based on my skills and market value, not my current underpaid situation."

**Why good:**
- ✅ Honest (builds trust)
- ✅ Provides context (not your fault)
- ✅ Preemptively addresses lowball
- ✅ Reanchors to market

## 📞 Round 2: After Technical Interviews (They Like You!)

### **Scenario 4: "We'd like to make you an offer of ₹22 lakhs"**

**❌ BAD Response:**
> "Okay, thank you! I accept!"

**Why bad:** You left ₹8-13L on the table (could've gotten ₹30L+)

---

**✅ GOOD Response (Negotiate Up):**
> "Thank you for the offer! I'm excited about the role and team. However, ₹22 lakhs is below my expectations. Based on my experience - 4+ years with microservices, Kafka, distributed systems, and performance optimization - I was expecting ₹30-32 lakhs. I also have another offer at ₹28 lakhs from [Company]. Can you match ₹30 lakhs?"

**Why good:**
- ✅ Express excitement (they know you want it)
- ✅ Provide reasoning (justify higher number)
- ✅ Mention competing offer (creates urgency)
- ✅ Specific counter (₹30L, not vague "more")

---

**Their Likely Response:**
> "₹30L is a stretch. Best we can do is ₹25 lakhs."

**Your Response:**
> "I appreciate you coming up to ₹25L. Could you meet me at ₹28 lakhs? That would make this an easy decision for me. Alternatively, can we structure it as ₹25L base + ₹3L performance bonus to reach ₹28L total?"

**Why good:**
- ✅ Shows willingness to compromise
- ✅ Offers creative solution (base + bonus)
- ✅ Still pushing for target

---

### **Scenario 5: "We can offer ₹30 lakhs. When can you start?"**

**❌ BAD Response:**
> "Great! I accept. I can start next week!"

**Why bad:** You didn't negotiate ANYTHING beyond base salary

---

**✅ GOOD Response (Negotiate Total Package):**
> "Thank you! ₹30L is in line with my expectations. Before I give you a final answer, I'd like to understand the complete package:
>
> 1. What's the base vs variable split?
> 2. Is there stock/ESOP? If yes, vesting schedule?
> 3. Joining bonus or relocation assistance?
> 4. Health insurance coverage?
> 5. Work from home policy?
> 6. Annual increment cycle?
> 7. When is my next review for promotion?
>
> I'm very interested, but want to ensure we're aligned on the complete picture."

**Why good:**
- ✅ Shows professionalism
- ✅ Signals you've done this before
- ✅ Total comp > base salary
- ✅ Buys you time to get other offers

---

**Common Scenarios:**

**Base vs Variable:**
- **Good:** ₹30L base + ₹2L variable (₹32L total)
- **Bad:** ₹22L base + ₹8L variable (risky, variable not guaranteed)
- **Negotiate:** "Can we shift more to base? I prefer ₹28L base + ₹2L variable"

**Stock/ESOP:**
- **Good:** ₹28L cash + ₹5L ESOP/year (₹33L total comp)
- **Negotiate:** "What's the 4-year vesting value? Can I get ₹2L more in cash instead?"

**Joining Bonus:**
- **Ask:** "Is there a joining bonus to help with relocation and initial costs?"
- **Typical:** ₹1-3L one-time (never hurts to ask!)

## 🎯 Universal Negotiation Principles

### **1. Never Accept First Offer**

**Why:** First offer is ALWAYS negotiable
- Companies expect 10-20% negotiation
- If they offer ₹25L, they can probably do ₹28L

**Always Counter:**
- Offer: ₹25L → Counter: ₹30L → Settle: ₹27-28L ✅

---

### **2. Use the "Flinch" Technique**

**Them:** "We can offer ₹22 lakhs"

**You:** [Pause for 3 seconds, look slightly disappointed]

**You:** "Hmm, I was expecting closer to ₹30 lakhs based on market rate..."

**Why good:**
- ✅ Non-verbal signal (they see disappointment)
- ✅ Awkward silence makes them uncomfortable
- ✅ They often come up immediately: "Well, maybe we can do ₹25L..."

---

### **3. Anchor High**

**Psychology:** First number sets the tone

**Wrong:** "I'm looking for ₹22-25L"
- **Result:** They offer ₹20L ("We're close to your range!")

**Right:** "I'm looking for ₹30-35L"
- **Result:** They offer ₹28L (still good!)

**Always anchor higher than your target**

---

### **4. Create Competition (Even if You Don't Have It)**

**Weak:** "I'm only talking to you"
- **Result:** No urgency, lowball offer

**Strong:** "I'm interviewing with Razorpay, PhonePe, and Swiggy, all targeting similar range"
- **Result:** Urgency, better offer

**Even if you only have 1 offer, say you're "in final rounds with 2 other companies"**

---

### **5. Be Willing to Walk Away**

**Most Powerful Negotiation Tool**

**Weak:** Accept bad offer because you're desperate
- **Result:** ₹22L (leaving ₹8L on table)

**Strong:** "Thanks for the offer of ₹22L. I appreciate it, but that's below my target. I'll need to decline and focus on other opportunities."
- **Result:** "Wait! Let me check with my manager... we can do ₹26L"

**Only works if you're truly willing to walk** (you have ₹10L backup, you're fine!)

---

### **6. Time is on Your Side**

**Don't rush:**
- "Can I have 3-5 days to think about the offer?"
- Use this time to get other offers
- Use other offers to negotiate up

**Create urgency for THEM:**
- "I have another offer deadline on Friday. Can you expedite?"
- They'll move faster to not lose you

## 💬 Scripts for Common Objections

### **Objection 1: "You're asking for too much"**

**Your Response:**
> "I understand it's above your initial budget. However, ₹30L is market rate for 4+ years backend engineers with my skill set. I've done my research on Glassdoor, Blind, and conversations with peers. Companies like Razorpay, PhonePe, and Cred are offering this range. I'm confident I'll deliver value that justifies this investment."

---

### **Objection 2: "We can only offer ₹25L for SDE-2 level"**

**Your Response:**
> "I appreciate the transparency. Would you consider evaluating me for SDE-3/Senior level instead? My 4+ years experience, work with 200+ microservices, and track record with performance optimization aligns well with senior responsibilities. What's the range for that level?"

**Translation:** If the level is wrong, change the level (not the comp)

---

### **Objection 3: "Our salary is fixed, no negotiation"**

**Your Response (Option A - Test it):**
> "I understand. However, I have an offer from [Company] at ₹30L and Razorpay is my preference. Is there truly zero flexibility? Perhaps on ESOP, joining bonus, or next review cycle?"

**Why good:** Most "fixed" salaries have 10-15% flex

**Your Response (Option B - Walk):**
> "I appreciate your transparency. Unfortunately, ₹22L is below my target and I'll need to pursue other opportunities. Thank you for your time, and I hope we can work together in the future."

**Why good:** They'll often "find" flexibility when you walk

---

### **Objection 4: "We need to see your current payslips"**

**Your Response:**
> "I'd prefer not to share payslips as it contains sensitive information. However, I'm happy to provide:
> - Employment verification letter
> - Reference from my manager
> - Portfolio of work
>
> My current compensation is ₹10L, which is below market (why I'm leaving). I'm hoping you'll evaluate based on my skills and market value, not my current underpaid situation. Companies like [X] and [Y] have made offers without requiring payslips."

## 🚨 Red Flags (Walk Away)

**If company does ANY of these, walk away:**

1. ❌ **Refuses to give salary range upfront**
   - "Let's first see if you're a fit, then we'll discuss salary"
   - **Translation:** They want to lowball after you're invested

2. ❌ **Aggressive lowballing**
   - You ask ₹30L, they offer ₹15L
   - **Translation:** They don't value you

3. ❌ **Bait and switch**
   - Recruiter says ₹30L, offer is ₹20L
   - **Translation:** Dishonest company

4. ❌ **Pressure tactics**
   - "Accept now or offer expires in 2 hours"
   - **Translation:** Manipulative, toxic culture

5. ❌ **Won't put offer in writing**
   - "We'll offer ₹30L" but won't send offer letter
   - **Translation:** Will renege later

## 🎯 Negotiation Checklist

**Before Negotiating:**
- [ ] Know your target: ₹28-35L
- [ ] Research company range (Glassdoor, Blind)
- [ ] Have 2-3 competing offers (or interviews)
- [ ] Prepare your achievements (200 microservices, 70% improvement)
- [ ] Practice scripts with friend

**During Negotiation:**
- [ ] Never reveal ₹10L without context
- [ ] Always counter first offer
- [ ] Ask for 3-5 days to decide
- [ ] Get everything in writing
- [ ] Negotiate total comp (not just base)

**Before Accepting:**
- [ ] Base salary ≥ ₹25L (minimum)
- [ ] Total comp ≥ ₹28L (target)
- [ ] Offer letter received in writing
- [ ] Understand: base, variable, ESOP, bonus
- [ ] Know next review cycle
- [ ] Comfortable with all terms
